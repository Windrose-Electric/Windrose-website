EDREvent Data Recorder — the vehicle black box that records speed, braking, and other data in the seconds around a crash event; covered by UN R141
EORIEconomic Operators Registration and Identification — the EU customs registration number assigned to companies importing/exporting goods; required for vehicle imports
E-markEuropean type approval mark for individual vehicle components (e.g. a horn, a mirror, a battery pack) — confirms that specific component meets a UN Regulation independently of the whole vehicle
FPS MobilityFederal Public Service Mobility and Transport — the Belgian government authority that issues Whole Vehicle Type Approvals; Belgian approvals carry the code "e6"
FUPFront Underrun Protection — the structural barrier under the front of the truck that prevents a car from sliding underneath in a head-on collision; covered by UN R93
GVWGross Vehicle Weight — the maximum total weight of the vehicle including load; N3 category means over 12 tonnes GVW
IDInformation Document — the formal technical dossier submitted to the approval authority for each regulation, describing the vehicle's design and how it meets the requirement; one ID per UN Regulation
LDWSLane Departure Warning System — alerts the driver when the vehicle drifts out of its lane without signalling; covered by UN R130
MOUMemorandum of Understanding — a signed statement of intent between two parties; not legally binding in the same way as a contract
N3EU vehicle category designation for heavy goods vehicles with a gross vehicle weight over 12 tonnes — the category that applies to the WH13 truck
NACENomenclature of Economic Activities — the EU standard classification system for business activities; Windrose needs to register vehicle assembly activity at BCE to satisfy NBN's production site requirement
NBNBureau de Normalisation — the Belgian national standards body that administers World Manufacturer Identifier (WMI) allocations on behalf of SAE International; they control whether YCB is released to Windrose
NDANon-Disclosure Agreement — a confidentiality contract preventing either party from sharing the other's confidential information
NSAINational Standards Authority of Ireland — the Irish approval authority (e24) that issued the battery pack E-mark certificate
NVNaamloze Vennootschap — Belgian public limited company, equivalent to a PLC (UK) or SA (France/Belgium French); Windrose Technology Antwerp NV is this type of company
OTAOver The Air — remote software update delivery via cellular or Wi-Fi connection; the primary update method covered by R156/SUMS
R + numberUN Regulation — an international vehicle safety standard developed by UNECE (United Nations Economic Commission for Europe) and adopted by member states; e.g. R155 = the cybersecurity regulation
RFIRequest for Inspection / Report of Findings — the ATS test document recording what was inspected and the outcome; found in each ATS project folder
RUPRear Underrun Protection — the structural barrier under the rear of the truck preventing cars from going underneath in a rear collision; covered by UN R58 — currently a complete gap in Windrose's documentation
RXSWINRegulation X Software Identification Number — a unique code that tracks exactly which software version is approved for which regulation on a specific vehicle; managed under R156/SUMS
SAESAE International — formerly the Society of Automotive Engineers; the US-based standards body that assigns World Manufacturer Identifiers (WMIs) globally through national bodies like NBN
SUMSSoftware Update Management System — the organisational framework for managing vehicle software updates safely throughout the product lifecycle; must be certified under UN R156
TARAThreat Analysis and Risk Assessment — the formal engineering process of identifying potential cyber threats to the vehicle's systems, assessing their likelihood and impact, and selecting security controls; required by R155 and ISO/SAE 21434
TPMSTyre Pressure Monitoring System — continuously monitors tyre pressure and alerts the driver if a tyre is under-inflated; covered by UN R142
TSTechnical Service — the independent organisation designated by a type approval authority to conduct physical tests and audits on vehicles and systems on the authority's behalf; DEKRA is the proposed TS for the Belgian WVTA
UBOUltimate Beneficial Owner — the natural person(s) who ultimately own or control a company; Belgian AML law requires a notarial declaration of UBO for all companies
V&VVerification and Validation — Verification confirms the design is correct (does it meet the specification?); Validation confirms it works in the real world (does it do what users need?); both required for R155 cybersecurity
VATValue Added Tax — Belgian consumption tax; a VAT registration certificate proves the company is registered as a Belgian taxable entity
VINVehicle Identification Number — the unique 17-character identifier stamped on every vehicle; the first 3 characters are the WMI (e.g. YCB for Windrose Technology Antwerp)
WMIWorld Manufacturer Identifier — the first 3 characters of every VIN, identifying the manufacturer and country; YCB has been allocated to Windrose Technology Antwerp NV by NBN but is currently withheld pending production site proof
WVTAWhole Vehicle Type Approval — the EU certificate issued under Regulation 2018/858 that allows a specific vehicle model to be legally sold and registered across all EU member states; the goal of this entire process

R155 Scope
CSMS Certificate — Scope Extension to Belgian Entity
Existing cert CYPRUS*CERT*1032*01 names Anhui Windrose Holdings only. To extend to Windrose Technology Antwerp NV, CETOC or DEKRA must audit the Belgian entity's cybersecurity management operations. Requires establishing and documenting CSMS processes at the Belgian level, then an on-site or documentary audit by the Technical Service. Cannot be done by paperwork alone.
Audit required
R156 Scope
SUMS Certificate — Scope Extension to Belgian Entity
Existing cert CYPRUS*CERT*1021*01 names Anhui Windrose Holdings only. Same process as R155 — requires TS audit of Belgian entity's software update management operations. The two audits can be combined into a single site visit.
Audit required
R58
Rear Underrun Protection (RUP)
Confirmed zero documentation in Drive. No Information Document, no ATS project folder, no test report, no E-mark — nothing. Rear underrun protection prevents a car from going under the rear of the truck in a collision. The Swedish WVTA lists R58 with all fields blank (dashes), suggesting it was either found not applicable or was missed. Must be resolved before Belgian approval: either (a) measure the WH13's rear structure geometry and formally document why it meets exemption criteria, or (b) commission full R58 testing with the Technical Service from scratch.
Zero docs
WMI YCB
World Manufacturer Identifier — Unblocking NBN Release
WMI code YCB was allocated to Windrose Technology Antwerp NV by NBN (Bureau de Normalisation). NBN is withholding the updated certificate pending proof of a real production site in Belgium. Without YCB confirmed, every VIN on the new WVTA cannot be issued. Resolution requires: (1) sign a lease for the Antwerp site at Houtdok-Noordkaai 16, (2) register vehicle assembly NACE code at BCE, (3) submit lease, BCE extract, and site photos to NBN. The circular dependency (WVTA requires WMI; NBN requires WVTA or production site) can only be broken by establishing the physical site.
